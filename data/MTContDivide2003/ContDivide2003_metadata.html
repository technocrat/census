Abstract:
Path of the Continental Divide through Montana. In Montana, the Continental Divide separates the watershed of the Columbia River from the watersheds of the Mississippi and Nelson Rivers. This data is based on 1:100,000 scale U.S. Geological Survey quadrangles. The layer is a series of 18 connected lines because the original data was in ESRI coverage format, which only supports 500 vertices per line feature.

Lineage:
Source information:
Originator: U.S Natural Resources Conservation Service
Publication date: 2003
Title: Montana 5th-code Watersheds
Publication place: Helena, MT
Publisher: Montana State Library
Online linkage: http://nris.mt.gov/nsdi/nris/hd109.html
Source scale denominator: 100000
Source contribution: The lines in the data set were selected from this source.

Process step:
Dissolve the hydrologic unit polygons based on the first two digits of their hydrologic unit codes. Delete lines that represent the state boundary. Generalize the lines with a tolerance of 10 meters. Edit the data to properly follow the Continental Divide between Mount Custer and the boundary with Canada.
